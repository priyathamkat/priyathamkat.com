--- layout: project title: "SEM Image Super-resolution" authors: "Priyatham Kattakinda" location: "KLA Corporation, Chennai, India" duration: "May 2019 - July 2019" category: "Internship" summary: " The quality of a scanning electron microscope (SEM) image is largely determined by the scan rate of the electon beam. Low scan rates produce high resolution images but take a long time. On the other hand, high scan rates are fast, but the image quality is poor. Any downstream tasks (such as defect detection) are also affected by this tradeoff. To alleviate this tradeoff, I synthesized a GAN-based super-resolving CNN that reproduces any defects in the input faithfully. Interestingly, the outputs from this network perform better than even the ground truths, presumably, due to the smoothing properties of the CNN.
